Key Observation
The June and September LEAPS puts on SPY/QQQ carry $25B+ notional — these aren't short-term hedges but structural portfolio protection positioned months out. This suggests institutional investors are hedging against a potential H2 2026 downturn.

Bottom Line

The Feb 26 options flow tells a two-track story. On one hand, institutional money is aggressively buying downside protection across indices (SPY, QQQ, IWM) and credit (HYG), with some of the most extreme put/call ratios in small-caps and high-yield. This is textbook late-cycle hedging behavior.

On the other hand, speculative call buying is concentrated in NFLX, crypto proxies (MSTR, MARA), precious metals (GLD, SLV), and defense (LMT). This suggests traders are positioning for sector rotation into perceived winners — companies with pricing power, hard-asset exposure, or government spending tailwinds.

The divergence between index-level fear and single-stock bullishness is the defining feature of this tape. The market isn't uniformly bearish or bullish — it's selectively hedged and selectively greedy, which historically characterizes transitional market regimes.
