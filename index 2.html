Bottom Line

The Feb 26 options flow tells a two-track story. On one hand, institutional money is aggressively buying downside protection across indices (SPY, QQQ, IWM) and credit (HYG), with some of the most extreme put/call ratios in small-caps and high-yield. This is textbook late-cycle hedging behavior.

On the other hand, speculative call buying is concentrated in NFLX, crypto proxies (MSTR, MARA), precious metals (GLD, SLV), and defense (LMT). Traders are positioning for sector rotation into perceived winners — companies with pricing power, hard-asset exposure, or government spending tailwinds.

The divergence between index-level fear and single-stock bullishness is the defining feature. The market isn't uniformly bearish or bullish — it's selectively hedged and selectively greedy, which historically characterizes transitional market regimes.
